Welcome to this experiment!

The following experiment is carried out as part of a seminar paper at Heidelberg University. Your personal data will be anonymized and not passed on.

You are about to contribute to the comparison of two voting mechanisms by voting on a number of policy proposals:

1. Majority Voting:

Everybody gets one vote per election, the majority of people decides.

2. Quadratic Voting:

Multiple votes can be cast for one issue. If a proposal receives more votes for then against, it will be implemented, regardless of the number of people who actually voted for/against it.
